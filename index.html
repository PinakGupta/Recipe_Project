Gulab Jamun


Ingredients

  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 cup of milk powder
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup of ghee (clarified butter)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • 2-3 tablespoons of milk (as needed to form a soft dough)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Recipe

  1. Prepare Sugar Syrup:

    • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water.
    • Bring it to a boil and simmer until it forms a slightly sticky syrup.
    • Add cardamom powder and saffron strands. Set aside.

  2. Make Dough:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee,
      and a pinch of baking soda.
    • Gradually add milk and knead to form a soft and smooth dough.
      Be cautious not to make it too sticky.

  3. Shape Gulab Jamun:

    • Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into smooth
      balls without any cracks. Ensure they are uniform in size.

  4. Heat Oil/Ghee:

    • Heat sufficient oil or ghee in a deep pan for deep frying over
      medium heat.

  5. Fry Gulab Jamun:

    • Gently slide the prepared balls into the hot oil.
    • Fry them on low to medium heat until they turn golden brown,
      ensuring they are evenly cooked.

  6. Soak in Sugar Syrup:

    • Once fried, drain excess oil and immediately transfer the hot
      gulab jamuns into the prepared sugar syrup.

  7. Allow to Soak:

    • Let the gulab jamuns soak in the sugar syrup for at least 1-2
      hours. They will absorb the syrup and become soft and flavorful.

  8. Serve:

    • Gulab Jamuns are ready to be served! You can garnish them with
      chopped nuts if desired.

Burfi


Ingredients

  • 1 cup of condensed milk
  • 1 cup of milk powder
  • 1/2 cup of ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 cup of finely chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • A pinch of cardamom powder

Recipe

  1. Prepare Sugar Syrup:

    • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water.
    • Bring it to a boil and simmer until it forms a one-string consistency syrup.
    • Add cardamom powder. Set aside.

  2. Mix Burfi Mixture:

    • In a separate pan, melt ghee over low heat.
    • Add condensed milk and stir continuously.
    • Gradually add milk powder and continue stirring to avoid lumps.
    • Add chopped nuts and mix well.

  3. Cook Burfi Mixture:

    • Cook the mixture on low heat until it thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.

  4. Set Burfi:

    • Transfer the mixture to a greased tray and spread it evenly.
    • Allow it to cool and set for a few hours.

  5. Cut and Serve:

    • Cut the set burfi into desired shapes.
    • Enjoy the delicious burfi!
